TRAINING

At Kirby Aquatics, we prepare lifeguards for the real-world. Lifeguarding is more than enforcing pool rules and getting a tan. In the US, an average of 7 children die each day from drowning in public pools.

Every Kirby Aquatics instructor has at least 20 years of front-line pool experience. We can leverage a wealth of expertise to teach you what lifeguarding is really like, because we've lived it. Our lessons come from the real world, not from a textbook.


FAQs


  • How long are the classes?
  • Normal certification classes are a week long. You will spend 5 evenings in the classroom, about 4 hours each evening. After that, we spend a whole day in the pool on the weekend, usually a Saturday. Sometimes we do offer compressed classes that take only 3 or 4 days, usually over holiday breaks.

  • Do I have to be an awesome swimmer?
  • The most important skill of a lifeguard is paying attention, not swimming. If you don't see the problem, no amount of swimming skill will help; however, it is important that you are comfortable in the water. We don't need varsity-level swimmers, but basic water skills are required. There are three swimming prerequisites to enter the class:

  • 1. Swim 300 yards continuously, without stopping and without touching the bottom.
  • 2. Starting in the water, swim 20 yards out, retrieve a brick in ~10 feet of water, and return the brick 20 yards to the side; all within 1 minute and 40 seconds.
  • 3. Tread water for 2 minutes using only legs, no hands.

  • How much does the class cost?
  • Certification classes are $290.

  • What happens if I fail?
  • If you fail, you may retake the class in its entirety one time, space available. After the second fail, you must restart the process, including full payment. No refunds will be offered under any circumstances.

  • How would I fail the class?
  • You must complete all of the following in order to pass. Failure to meet any of these requirements will mean an automatic fail:

  • - You must complete the prerequisites listed above, as well as all water skills taught in the class.

  • - You must attend every minute of every class. No exceptions.

  • - You must pass written, multiple-choice tests covering the class materials.

  • - You must be able to complete all land-based skills (First Aid, CPR, etc.)

  • - Your participation in all classes must be respectful and enthusiastic.

  • Can I hire Kirby Aquatics lifeguards?
  • We do not offer lifeguard placement, but please contact us if you are looking for highly-qualified lifeguards. We will pass your info on to guards in need of positions.

  • How long does the certification last?
  • Your certification will last 2 years; however, you must maintain your skills and readiness during those two years.

  • How is Kirby Aquatics able to certify lifeguards?
  • Kirby Aquatics follows the American Red Cross lifeguarding program, as well as meeting or exceeding all standards set by the United States Lifeguard Standards Coalition.

  • What is included in the lifeguard certification class? Does Kirby Aquatics offer other classes?
  • The Kirby Aquatics certification class includes Basic Lifeguarding, First Aid, CPR and AED certifications in one class. At this time, we do not offer any of these certifications alone, nor do we offer ancillary certifications, such as lifeguard instructor certification, lifeguarding refresher courses, etc.

  • Does Kirby Aquatics offer online learning options?
  • No. At Kirby Aquatics, we believe the best way to learn is through interaction with experts and hands-on activity. We highly encourage participants to review the materials ahead of time, but all classes will be on-site, in-person.
